MADDEN MOBILE BEGINNERS DEFENSIVE STRATEGY GUIDE

 

 

Defense wins games – and in H2H and LvL it can be the difference in winning or losing.   This is a beginner’s guide to help understand defensive formations and along with understanding what stats to look for will help you choose the best defensive strategy.   First off it is noticeable that there are 14 player slots to be filled but only 11 will ever be on the field at a time.   The 11 players that will be on the field depend on which defensive formation you choose.   

The picture above shows labels along with a picture of the defense in Madden Mobile.  From the offensive perspective the picture is flipped.  So Cb1 will be on the right-hand side of the screen.

Labels: CB1 – cornerback 1, FS – free safety, ROLB – right outside linebacker, MLB – middle linebacker, LOLB – left outside linebacker, SS – strong safety, CB2 – cornerback 2, Nickel – nickelback, DE1 – also called the weak defensive end, DT1 – defensive tackle1, DE2 – strong defensive end, Dime – dimeback

Defensive Linemen (DL) will refer to the DE1, DT1, 4-3 DT, DE2.

Linebackers (LB) will refer to ROLB, MLB, 3-4 LB, LOLB.    OLB will refer to both ROLB & LOLB

Defensive backs (DB) will refer to CB1, FS, SS, CB2, Nickel and Dime.

How the Defensive Strategy works:

This will be updated if it changes in new season, but currently there are two choices in your defense.  You can set a defensive strategy or let the AI choose for you.  Most people who play in leagues competitively have a defensive strategy.  There are only 3 drives and 1 point can determine a win. The defensive AI can take a while to adjust to the plays being run, as well as players typically have a few go-to plays – you can find this out by looking at the plays they consistently run on 3rd / 4th and long.  So having defensive plays to counter them can help you gain the edge to win.  As well as that in H2H ties are determined by whoever scores the fastest.  If your defensive strategy causes a player to take longer to score, then it benefits you.

As you can tell, the defensive strategy is segmented into down and distance.  You can choose up to 5 plays in each down and distance.  Whatever plays you put into the down and distance are the only plays that are going to be used.  If you have 1 play, that is the only play that is going to be used.  If the down and distance is empty, then the AI will choose what play to pick.  How many plays to have in each is up to personal preference – some people prefer 2 others prefer the entire 5.  Some people have the same five plays for all down and distance. Just remember that currently, 1st down doesn’t have a distance signifier meaning that first and goal will still choose plays in first down playbook.

When looking at the plays, it is from the offensive perspective.   If a play shows a CB blitzing from the right, the opponent will see the CB blitzing from the right.   Finally, any play that is selected will show up in the Favorites Tab, where you can easily manage the plays you have chosen.


 

 

Play Types:

There are four types of defense: Man, Zone, Blitz, Special Teams. 

Man coverage is where each defensive player covers one specific offensive player and will follow them throughout the entirety of the play.   Man gets hurt by mismatches in speed and good route running.   How well a player is at Man coverage is shown on the card as “Man”

Zone is where defenders play a specific zone on the field – when an offensive player runs from one zone to another, they will stop being covered by one player and then be covered by another.  Zone is less prone to get hurt by speed however there are “holes’ in coverage where the defenders switch.  How well a player is at Zone coverage is shown as “zon”

Blitz has a mixture of plays from Man and Zone.  It shows plays that have 5 or more defenders rushing the quarterback.  Sending extra defenders rushing at the QB causes extra pressure on the QB but also leaves more receivers open – so it can either help in causing a mistake or turn what would have been a small gain into a rather large gain.

Special Teams are plays used for blocking field goals, puts, kick returns, etc.

 

Formation:

Now if you look at the different defensive plays via Man, Zone, Blitz, there is a long list of different plays and they can be hard to distinguish.   To help choose the best plays, go to “formation.”  There are 7 different formations: 3-4, 4-3, Nickel, Dime, Quarter, Goal Line, Special teams.   This document won’t get into special teams unless requested – maybe in an intermediate guide going over what plays do, what press does and what the different colors represent.

When choosing plays, you want to look at what players are in that formation and what they will be doing.   There are four cover2 plays, one for 3-4, 4-3, Nickel and Dime, each with different personnel. Using the following information, you can choose the plays best suit your team.   You also want to use this information to see where players are going and choose the best plays for you.

 

 

For ease of use, most formations will be generalized to have a front, a middle and a back.  The front is nearest to line of scrimmage and is where the Dline will be located.  Middle is typically where the linebackers will be and is the middle of the play art.  Back is typically where the safeties will be and is furthest from the line of scrimmage.  Players enclosed by a yellow square are the ones used for the formation. The number on the bottom right of each formation is how many plays it contains.

 

3-4

The 3-4 Formation Has the top 4 DBs, the 4 LBs and 3 DL.  Typically, the one of the OLBs are near the front with the 3 Defensive Linemen.  The MLB and 3-4 LB are in the middle with the CBs covering the outside receivers.  The Safeties are in the back.  This formation is versatile if you have several LBs and is useful for stopping the run, since OLBs will typically have better speed than the DL.

 

4-3

The 4-3 Formation has 4 DBs, 3 LB and 4 DL.  The linemen will be the front four rushing the QB, while the linebackers will be in the middle and the defensive backs will be in the back field.  Some plays have where an OLB is on the line.   This is a multipurpose formation and can be used in for run and pass coverage.

 

 

Nickel

The Nickel formation has the top 4 DBs, the nickel DB, 2 LB and the 4 DL.  Typically, the lineup will be similar to 4-3, but where the Nickel will be taking the spot of the ROLB.  This formation is useful in stopping passes as the Nickel will typically have better hands and coverage stats than the ROLB.   You also typically want a strong nickel to help stop the run.


 

 

Dime

The Dime formation has the top 4 DBs, the Nickel and Dime DBs, 1 LB and 4 DL.  This is also similar to 4-3 with the dime and nickel taking the place of the OLBs.  This formation is very useful in helping stop passing plays with the drawback of being weaker at blitzing and stopping the run.

 

 

Quarter:

The Quarter formation has 6 DBs, 2 LB and 3 DL.  In this formation in the front are the 3DL.  In the middle are the LBs along with the CBs on the outside covering the receivers.  At the back are the Safeties and Nickel.  This formation is great at stopping long passes but is weak at stopping passes near the line of scrimmage and is the weakest at stopping the run.  While not used much, it can cause frustration for opponents who are pass heavy.

 

 

Goal Line

Goal Line formation has all 4 DL and the OLBs at the front. In the middle are the MLB and 3-4 LB with the CBs on the outside against any receivers.   This is primarily used for stopping plays when the opponent is on the 5-yard line. This formation is lighter and typically has very high tackle and hit power. It is great against the run but since there are no safeties, the weakest against the pass – so best not use it in situations where the opponent can gain a large amounts of yards,
